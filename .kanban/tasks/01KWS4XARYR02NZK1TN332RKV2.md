Every test in ConnectionTests.swift called connection.close() manually as its last line; when a request threw before reaching that line (the already-documented flaky 30s timeout in requestReceivesItsScriptedTypedResponse(), or any other throw), close() never ran. close() is the only thing that kills the real child process and unblocks the two detached reader/stderr loops, which read via a raw blocking read(2) outside actor isolation and never unblock until close() kills the process. Each leaked loop permanently pins one thread of Swift's fixed-size cooperative concurrency thread pool for the rest of the binary's life — enough leaking (it only took the one known flake, once) eventually starves the pool and wedges the entire test binary, not just this suite, well after ConnectionTests itself finished reporting.
